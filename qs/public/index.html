Queer Definition

Queer is an umbrella term for people who are not heterosexual or cisgender. In history, The word "Queer" has been used to identofy something strange or odd. This is why there was a ongoing discussion about the word "queer". In the late 1980's, this word has been embraced by queer activists such as the Queer Nation. They have reclaimed the word and translated something into a progresive word. Nowadays, people in LGBTQ+ community has been using this word a lot. Currently, the word queer has been used to identify a broad spectrum of sexual and gender identities.

First Queer Movements

After world war 2, homosexual activist groups has been starting to be heard. In the late 1940's in Denmark and Netherlands, the homophile movements began. In 1965 gay march held in front of Independence Hall in Philadelphia. This movement was so powerful and effective that some considered this movements as the beginning of the modern gay right movements. Meanwhile there were some activist groups being formed in San Fransisco. This group is called LGBT youth organization Vanguard. They have been fighting for equal rights for LGBTQ+ community around 1966.

Amber Fears



That lifestyle is a sin.

Our mothers told us when we were little.

What caused your homosexuality?

We quietly cower: we feel brittle.


“Man shall not lay with man”,

But what about the women?

The church says Hell is for us.

We ask God: Am I forgiven?


They say we're mad at them,

With their compulsory heterosexuality.

Apparently lesbianism is a result of men.

We roll our eyes: they will know the actuality.
